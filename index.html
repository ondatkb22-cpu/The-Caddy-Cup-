    🐊 The V7 Projection Engine

    A fully-fused model stitching DraftKings salaries, DataGolf's CH model team probabilities, DataGolf's stat decomposition with course fit, and a 50,000-trial Monte Carlo simulation with team-correlated residuals.

    📐 Pipeline

    1. Sources

    DK salaries (148 players), DG field (74 teams), DG decomposition (SG forecast + σ), CH model (team win/T5/T10/T20/cut%)

    →
    2. Project

    Expected DK points = scoring EV (SG × multiplier × expected rounds) + finish EV (weighted bonus from CH probs)

    →
    3. Simulate

    50k trials with teammate correlation ρ=0.65, scoring ~ N(EV, σ·√rounds) + categorical finish bonuses

    →
    4. Optimize

    K-best knapsack (top-40 per objective) across 6 value functions with strict 1-per-team. Every candidate scored through MC.

    →
    5. Rank

    GPP: 0.4·mean + 0.35·p90 + 0.25·p99. Cash: 0.6·mean + 0.4·p25

    ⚙️ Parameters

    • Scoring multiplier: 3.0 DK pts per 1.0 SG/round — from DK scoring rules (a +1 SG round ≈ +2 birdies = +6 raw pts, diminished by par/bogey variance to ~3)
    • Base round: 9.0 pts — par-shooting baseline (15 pars + 2 birdies + 1 bogey ≈ 9)
    • Expected rounds: 4·P(cut) + 2·(1−P(cut))
    • Team correlation: ρ = 0.65 — teammates share course, weather, format, and one card (alt-shot) of every 2 rounds
    • Finish bonus breakdown: Win +30, T5 +14, T10 +7, T20 +5, T21-T30 +2 — full DK payout table
    • T30 approximation: P(T20) + 0.25·(P(cut) − P(T20)) — CH model doesn't publish T30 directly; this matches historical Tour ratio of cut-maker finishes landing T21-T30

    🎯 Backtest Findings

    Under the DK 1-per-team rule (only one player from each pair is allowed), 67 candidate lineups were generated across 6 value functions (pure projection, ceiling-tilted at 0.5σ and 0.75σ, floor-tilted, win-probability-weighted, linear win boost) and scored through 50,000 Monte Carlo trials:

    • Matt Fitzpatrick (T41) is in every single top-30 lineup — his 1.87 SG/rd DG forecast is 0.8 SG ahead of the #2 player (Thorbjornsen at 1.07), giving him a ~10-point projection gap. He is a non-negotiable chalk lock.
    • Michael Brennan is the highest-value play at $8,200: 42.8 projection = 5.22 pts/$1k, best non-Fitz ratio in the field. He appears in 90%+ of top GPP lineups.
    • The #1 GPP lineup pairs Fitz with Brennan + Clark + Stevens + Dumont de Chassart + Blades Brown — MC mean 251.2, p90 302.3, p99 346.3, all six from distinct pairs.
    • Top NON-Fitz pivot (for tournament leverage when fading chalk): Gerard + Theegala + Stevens + Brennan + McGreevy + Brown — MC mean 245.4 (only −5.8 pts vs the chalky #1). With Fitz projected at near 100% ownership, this lineup captures meaningful upside if Fitz misses the cut or underperforms.
    • GPP and Cash lineups overlap heavily: under strict 1-per-team, the ceiling-tilted and floor-tilted optimizers converge (top 5 of each share 4+ players). Cash just swaps Brown for lower-variance options; GPP swaps in more win-prob upside.

    ⚠️ Model Caveats

    • DK rule enforced: only one player per pair. The optimizer and all pre-computed lineups respect this hard constraint.
    • CH model probabilities are the ground truth for team placement — any error there propagates to finish EV.
    • DG σ is SG-per-round volatility, translated to DK points via the same 3× multiplier as the mean. True volatility likely has fatter tails (hole-in-ones, bogey-free bonuses) not modeled.
    • Ownership / leverage is not in this dataset. GPP ranking optimizes for raw ceiling, not for field-adjusted EV. For tournaments, manually fade overly chalky Matt Fitzpatrick exposure in MME builds.
    • No wind/weather adjustment on top of DG's baseline — if conditions change significantly Thursday, refresh DG decomposition.
